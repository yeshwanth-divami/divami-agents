# Doc-Narrator Writing Rules

These rules apply to every file this skill produces. They are non-negotiable structural patterns, not style preferences.

---

## Context Seed

Every document opens with 3-5 sentences that make it self-contained. The seed appears before any heading.

Must answer:
1. What project is this about? (one sentence)
2. What is this specific document? (one sentence)
3. What will the reader understand by the end? (one sentence)

**Test:** Cover the title. Show the seed to someone who has never seen this project. Can they follow the document? If yes, the seed works.

---

## Prose Before Structure

Tell the story in prose before showing any table, list, or diagram. The reader must know what they are about to see before they see it.

---

## Open Questions Are First-Class

Every document has an Open Questions section at the end. Each item:
- Numbered
- States the question clearly
- States options if known
- States why it is blocked or deferred

If there are no open questions, write "None." Do not omit the section.

---

## Inline Links

Link to other documents at the moment the reader's curiosity arises — not in a footer block. The link appears because the narrative led there.

---

## Plain Language

Write so a motivated reader can follow without re-reading sentences. Cut nominalizations, hollow adjectives, and long compound sentences.

| Foggy | Clear |
|---|---|
| "AI-powered case structuring" | "AI structures your case" |
| "performance improvement<br/>was achieved" | "performance improved" |
| "advanced participant<br/>management" | "control who joins" |
