# Audience: Non-Technical

Executives, product managers, or domain stakeholders who need to understand what a system does and why — not how it is built.

## Writing defaults

- Lead with business impact, not system mechanics. The first paragraph answers: "What does this mean for us?"
- No jargon without an inline plain-language definition. If you need the glossary link, the term is too technical — reword or define it in the sentence.
- Analogies over acronyms. If you can say what something is *like*, do that before saying what it *is*.
- No code blocks. No sequence diagrams with more than 3 participants.
- Decision-first structure: what changed → what it means → what to do next.
- Shorter paragraphs: 3 sentences max before a visual break.
- Omit a dedicated justification table unless the audience will be making the enumerated decisions themselves. Replace it with a one-sentence justification inline.
- Open questions should be phrased as business decisions, not technical ones.

## Signals that trigger this audience

Explicit: `exec`, `non-tech`, `plain language`, `stakeholder`, `for leadership`, `for PM`
